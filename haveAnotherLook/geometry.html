Distance

Distance from a point to a line is the length of the shortest line from the point to any point on line

  • part of the line (BF), the perpendicular from the point to the line (AE) and any other line between the point and the line (AF) will form a triangle
  • ∠ AEF and ∠ EFC must both be < 90 degrees (sum of the angles of a triange = 180)
  • ∴ ∠ AEF is the triangle's largest angle
  • ∴ AF is the longest side
  • ∴ AE is the shortest distance between the point and the line

Parallel lines: equidistant (in the same plane) and never meet

Transversal of Parallel Lines

  • The 2 angles labelled A are equivalent; otherwise the two horizontal lines would not be parallel
  • furthermore, A=C and B=D
    • B = 180 - A
    • C = 180 - B = 180 -(180 - A) = A

Side-Side-Side Theorem (Euclid's Elements Book1, Proposition 7)

Proof by contradiction:

  1. Suppose there is a second triangle ADC and AD = AC and CB = DB
  2. then triangles CAD an CBD are isosceles
  3. thus ∠ ACD = ∠ CDA and ∠ BCD = ∠ CDB
  4. ∠ ACD > ∠ BCD (by containment)
  5. ∠ ACD > ∠ CDB (see 3)
  6. ∠ CDA > ∠ CDB (see 3)
  7. but ∠ CDA < ∠ CDB (by containment)
  8. therefore, there is no such second triange
  9. therefore, △ = △ ABC
  10. therefore, ∠ ABC = ∠ ABC and ∠ BCA = ∠ BDC and ∠ CAB = ∠ DAB
