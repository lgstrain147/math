Possible Derivations
Coil Rope [1]

Coil rope 1 unit in diameter by 64 units long makes a circle 9 units wide

Cylindrical Containers [2]

May have compared the volumn of cylindrical container to a rectangular containers of the same height with a square bases.

Mancala (Circles in a circle) [3]

Circle of diameter 9 can be filled with 64 circles of diameter 1 which can be rearranged into an 8x8 square ie.

  • 1 white circle in the middle
  • surrounded by 6 grey circles
  • surrounded by 13 red circles
  • surrounded by 19 green circles
  • surrounded by 25 white circles
  • for a total of 64 circles which can be rearranged into 8x8 square
  • therefore, area of circle ~= area of square which is equal to 64 units.

Using Pythagorus

Area of Inscribed (blue) Square
  • 2(AD)2 = (AE)2
  • ∴ √ 2 AD = AE = r
  • ∴ AD = r / √ 2
  • ∴ Area
    • = (2AD)2
    • = [2r / (√ 2)]2
    • = 2 r2
    • ie π = 2 which is off by ~36%
Area of Red Square
[4]
  • = (2AB)2
  • (2 √ 3 / 2 r)2
    • since:
      • r = AC and
      • AB = √ 3 / 2 AC (Pythagorun)
  • = 3 r2
  • ∴ π = 3 which is off by ~ 15%

Approximate with Hexagaon

Inscribed Hexagon

  • = 12 * area △ ABC
  • = 12 * 1/2 AC * BC
  • = 6 * √ 3 / 2 r * 1/2 r
    • since:
      • r = AB
      • AC = √ 3 /2 AB and BC = 1/2 AC (Pythagorus)
  • = 3/2 √ 3 r2
  • ∴ π = 3/2 √ 3 = ~ 2.6 which is off by ~ 17.3%

Inscribing Hexagaon

  • = 12 * area △ ADE
  • = 12 * 1/2 AD * DE
  • = 6 * r * r / √ 3
    • since:
      • r = AD
      • DE =AD / √ 3 (Pythagorus)
  • = 6 / √ 3 r2
  • ∴ π = 6 / √ 3 = ~ 3.46 which is off by ~ 10%

Approximate with Octagon

Simple Approach:

7/9 of area of d x d square where d is the diameter of the circle

  • = 7/9(2r)2
  • ~= 3.11 r2
  • ie. π ~= 3.11 which is off by ~1%

Inscribed Octagon

  • = 16 * area △ ABC
  • = 16 * 1/2 AB * BC
  • = 8 * cos(360/16) r * sin(360/16) r
  • = 8 cos(360/16) * sin(360/8) * r2
  • ∴ π = 8 cos(360/16) sin(360/16) = u 2.83 which is off by ~ 10%

Inscribing Octagon

  • = 16 * area △ ADE
  • = 16 * 1/2 AD * DE
  • = 8 * r * sin(360/16)/cos(360/16) r
    • since:
      • r = AD
      • DE = sin(360/16)/cos(360/16) AD (Pythagorus)
  • = 8 * sin(360/16)/cos(360/16) r2
  • ∴ π = 8 * sin(360/16)/cos(360/16) = ~ 3.31 which is off by ~ 5.5%

Generally, for a N-sided Polygon

Inscribed Polygon

  • = n cos(360/2n) * sin(360/2n) * r2

Inscribing Polygon

  • = n * sin(360/2n)/cos(360/2n) r2

Method of Exhaustion (Ancient Greece 5th and 4th centuries BC)

  • conceived of by Antiphon in Greece in mid 5th century BC
    • area of inscribed regular polygon approaches area of circle as the number of sides increases
  • Expanded upon by Bryson of Heraclea in late 5th century BC
  • Formalized by Eudoxus in early 4th century BC

Calculus

  • lim n-> ∞ n cos(2π/2n) * sin(2π/2n) * r2
  • lim n-> ∞ n sin(π/n) * r2
  • lim n-> ∞ n * π/n [sin(π/n)]/(π/n) * r2
  • lim n-> ∞ π [sin(π/n)]/(π/n) * r2
  • πr2 lim n-> ∞ [sin(π/n)]/(π/n)
  • πr2
    • since:
      • limit x->0 sinx/x = 1 [5]
      • Proof:
        • AC = 1
        • area △ ADC <= ⌔ ADC <= △ ADE
        • 1/2 AD sin(θ) <= θ / 2π * π <= 1/2 AD DE
        • sin(θ) <= θ <= DE
        • sin(θ) <= θ <= tan(θ)
          • since:
            • tan(θ) = DE/AD and
            • AD = 1
        • sin(θ) <= θ <= sin(θ)/cos(θ)
        • 1 <= θ/sin(θ) <= 1/cos(θ)
        • 1 >= sin(θ)/θ) >= cos(θ)
        • lim θ -> 0 1 >= lim θ -> 0 sin(θ)/θ) >= lim θ -> 0 cos(θ)
        • 1 >= lim θ -> 0 sin(θ)/θ) >= 1
        • lim θ -> 0 sin(θ)/θ) = 1

Circumference

  • = lim n->∞ n 2r sin(2π / 2n)
  • = lim n->∞ n 2r π/n sin(π)/(π/n)
  • = 2πr
