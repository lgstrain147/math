Pythagorus Theorem

AC2 = AB2 + BC2

Proof

  • ∠ ABD = 90° - ∠ DBC
  • and since the angles of a triangle add up to 180°, ∠ BCD also = 0° - ∠ DBC
  • similarly, ∠ DAB = ∠ DBC
  • ∴ triangles ABD, BCD and ABC are similar
  • ∴ AD /AB = AB / AC and DC / BC = BC / AC
  • ∴ AD = AB2 / AC and DC = BC2 / AC
  • but AC = AD + DC
  • ∴ AC = AB2 / AC + BC2 / AC
  • AC2 = AB2 + BC2
