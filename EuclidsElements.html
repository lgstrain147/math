Book 1: Plane Geometry involving Straight Lines

Definitions

  1. Point
  2. Line
  3. Perpendicular Lines and Right Angles: intersecting lines with equal angles

  4. Obtuse Angle: > right angle
  5. Acute Angle: < right angle
  6. Circle: all points equidistant from center point
  7. Rectilinear figures: contained by straight lines
    • Triangle: 3 straight lines
    • Quadrilateral: 4 straight lines
    • Multilataeral: more than 4 straight lines
  8. Triangles:
    • Equilateral: 3 equal sides
    • Isoceles Triangle: 2 equal side
    • Scalene Triangle: 3 unequal sides
  9. Triangles:
    • Right Angle
    • Obtuse
    • Acute: 3 acute angles
  10. Quadrilaterals:
    • Square: right angle and equilateral (equal length sides)
    • Rectangle: right angle and not equilateral
    • Rhombus: not right angle and equilateral
    • Rhomboid: opposite sides equal
    • Trapezia: other
  11. Parallel Lines: do not intersect

Postulates: assumptions / basis for reasoning

  1. Any 2 points can be connected by a (unique) straight line. (Uniqueness is not explicitly stated but assumed when using this postulate.)
  2. A straight line can be extended indefinitely
  3. A circle can be formed with any line segment as its radius
  4. Right angles are equal to one another
  5. If sum of internal angles on the same side of a line and two others add < 2 right angles, other lines meet on that side of the line

    Comment: This is exceptionally unjustified.

Common Notions

  1. Things equal to the same thing as equal to each other
  2. Equal things added to equal things produce equal wholes
  3. Equal things subtracted from equal things produce equal remainders
  4. Things coinciding with each other are equal
  5. The whole is greater than a part

Proposition 4 - Side Angle Side Theorem

If AB = DE and BC = EF and ∠ BAC = ∠ EDF, then triangles are congrugent

  • superimpose ∠ BAC on ∠ EDF
  • points A and D will coincide as will C and F
  • ∴ BC = EF (Postulate 1)
  • and ∠ ABD = ∠ DEF, ∠ BCA = ∠ EFC, ∠ CAB = ∠ FDE and △ ABC ≅ △ DEF (Common Notion 4)

Proposition 5 - Isosceles Triangles

The angles opposite the equal sides are equal

  1. △ ABC is isosceles.
  2. and AB = AC (Definition 20)
  3. extend AB and AC (Postulate 2)
  4. add points D and E such that BD = CE (Proposition 3)
  5. AD = AB + AD = AC + CE = AE (Common Notion 2)
  6. add lines DC and BE (Postulate 1)
  7. ∴ △ ABE ≅ △ ACD and BE = CD and ∠ BEA = ∠ CDA and ∠ ABE = ∠ ACD (SAS)
  8. ∴ △ BDC ≅ △ CEB and ∠ DCB = ∠ EBC (SAS)
  9. and ∠ ABE = ∠ ACD (7)
  10. ∴ ∠ ABE - ∠ EBC = ∠ ACD - ∠ DCB (Common Notion 3)
  11. ∴ ∠ ABC = ∠ ACB (Common Notion 1)

Proposition 7 - Side Side Side

If AC = AD and BC = BD then C = D

Proof by contradiction:
  • Suppose there is a second triangle DBC and AB = DB and AC = DC
  • add line AD (Postulate 1)
  • ∴ triangles ABD an ACD are isosceles (Definition 20)
  • ∴ ∠ BAD = ∠ BDA and ∠ CAD = ∠ CDA (Proposition 5)
  • ∠ BAD   >   ∠ CAD   =   ∠ CDA   >   ∠ BDA   =   ∠ BAD (Common Notion 5)
  • but ∠ BAD cannot be > ∠ BAD
  • ∴ △ ABC ≅ △ DBC

Proposition 9 - Cut an angle in half

Proposition 10 - Cut a line in half

  • Construct equilateral triangle ABC (P1)
  • Cut ∠ ACB in half (P9)
  • ∴ △ ACD = △ BCD (SAS)
  • ∴ AD = BD

Proposition 13 - A line stood on another line makes angles which sum to 2 right angles
  • If line (EC) makes angles ACE and ECB and ∠ ACE = ∠ ECB then ∠ ACE and ∠ ECB are right angles (Definition 10)
  • Another line ie. DC makes angles ACD and DCB
  • ∠ ACD + ∠ DCB = (∠ ACE + ∠ ECD) + (∠ ECB - ∠ ECD) = ∠ ACE + ∠ ECB

Proposition 14

If ∠ CBA + ∠ ABD sum to 2 right angles, CD is a straight line.

  • Extend CB to D (P13)
  • ∠ CBA + ∠ ABD = ∠ CBA + ∠ ABE (CN1)
  • ∴ ∠ ABD = ∠ ABE (CN3)
  • but ∠ ABD   >   ∠ ABE (by construction)
  • ∴ CD is a straight linei (proof by contradiction

Proposition 15

If 2 straight lines intersect, they make opposite angles equal to each other.

Proof
  • ∠ AED + ∠ DEB = 2 right angles (P13)
  • and ∠ DEB + ∠ BEC = 2 right angles (P13)
  • ∴ ∠ AED + ∠ DEB = ∠ DEB + ∠ BEC (CN1)
  • ∴ ∠ AED + ∠ DEB - ∠ DEB = ∠ DEB + ∠ BEC - ∠ DEB (CN3)
  • ∴ ∠ AED = ∠ BEC
  • Similarly, ∠ AEC = ∠ DEB
  • ∴ ∠ ABC   <   ∠ AED

Proposition 16

When one of the sides of a triange is extended, it creates an external angle bigger than either of the opposite interior angles.

  • Place point E at the midpoint of AC (P10)
  • Extend line BE to F such that BE = EF (Post2,P3)
  • ∠ AEB = ∠ FEC (P15)
  • ∴ △ AEB = ∠ CEF and ∠ BAE = ∠ FCA (SAS)
  • and ∠ FCA   <   ∠ DCA (CN5)
  • ∴ ∠ BAE   <   ∠ DCA
  • similarly, ∠ ABC = ∠ HCG
  • and ∠ HCG = ∠ ICD (P15)
  • ∴ ∠ ABC = ∠ ICD (CN1)
  • and ∠ ICD   <   ∠ ACD (CN5)
  • ∴ ∠ ABC   <   ∠ ACD

Proposition 17

The sum of any 2 angles of a triangle is less than 2 right angles.

  • In diagram for P16, ∠ ACD > ∠ ABC (P16)
  • ∴ ∠ ACD + ∠ BCA   >   ∠ ABC + ∠ BCA
  • 2 right angles &nbps > &npsb ∠ ABC + ∠ BCA (P13)
  • similarly, 2 right angles &npsb > &nbps ∠ CAB + ∠ BCA ...

Proposition 18

In a triangle the longest Side Subtends the Biggest Angle

  • If AC is the longest side, add point D such that AD = AB (P3)
  • ∠ ADB = ∠ ABD (P5)
  • in △ DCB, ∠ DCB   <   ∠ ADB (P16)
  • ∴ ∠ DCB   <   ∠ ABD
  • ∴ ∠ ACB   <   ∠ ABC (CN5)
  • similarly, ∠ CAB   <   ∠ ABC

Proposition 19 - In a triangle, the bigger angle subtends the Biggest Side

Suppose ∠ ABC   >   ∠ BCA and ∠ ABC   >   ∠ BCA and suppose

  1. AC = AB
    • ∴ ∠ ABC = ∠ BCA (P%)
    • ∴ AC ~= AB (proof by contradiction)
  2. AC < AB
    • ∠ BCA   >   ∠ ABC (P18)
    • ∴ AC > AB (proof by contradiction)

Proposition 26 - Angle Angle Side

If triangles have two angles amd a side that are equal, the triangles are equal.

  • Suppose ∠ ABC = ∠ DEF and ∠ BCA = ∠ EFD> and
    1. BC = EF
      • if AB > DE, then add point G on AB such that GB = DE (P3)
      • △ GBC = △ DEF and ∠ GCB = ∠ ACB (SAS)
      • but ∠ ACB   >   ∠ GCB (CN5)
      • ∴ AB = DE (proof by contradiction)
      • ∴ △ ABC = △ DEF (SAS)
    2. or AB = DE
      • if BC > EF, then add point H on BD such that BH = EF (P3)
      • △ ABH = △ DEF and ∠ BAH = ∠ BAC (SAS)
      • but ∠ BAC   >   ∠ BAH (CN5)
      • ∴ BC = EF (proof by contradition)
      • ∴ △ ABC = △ DEF (SAS)

    Proposition 28

    If a line falling across 2 straight lines makes:

    1. the external angle equal to the internal and opposite angle on the same side
    2. or the sume of the internal angles on the same side equal to 2 right angles
    then the 2 straight lines are parallel

    Proposition 29 - If line across parallel lines makes equal alternate angles

    Proof by Contradiction
    • Suppose ∠ AGH   >   ∠ GHD
    • ∴ ∠ AGH + ∠ BGH   >   ∠ GHD + ∠ BGH (Common Notion 2)
    • 2 right angles >   ∠ GHD + ∠ BGH (Proposition 13)
    • ∴ AB intersects CD (Postulate 5)
    • but AB ∥ CD and therefore, AB does not intersect CD (Definition 23)
    • ∴ ∠ AGH = ∠ GHD
