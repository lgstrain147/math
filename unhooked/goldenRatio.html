Golden Ratio (ϕ) and Fibonacci Sequence

Golden Ratio

Definition

ϕ = L/S = L+S / L

Derivation

L2 = S (L+S)

L2 - SL - S2 = 0

L = [S +/ √(S2 + 4S2)]/2 according to the quadratic formula

L = [S +/ √(S2 x 5)]/2

L = [S + S√ 5]/2

L = [S x (1 + √ 5)]/2

L/S = (1 + √ 5)/2 = 1.618 = ϕ

Fibonacci Sequence

0,1,1,2,3,5,8,11,19,30,...

tn+1 = tn + tn-1

Used by Pingala in India in 200BC to count possible combinations of patterns of long (2 beat) and short (1 beat) syllables in lines of Sanskrit poetry consisting of n beats (Chandahsastra)

Length Patterns
1 S
2 SS
L

LS
SG
n+1 all patterns for n followed by S
all patterns for n-1 followed by G

Golden Ratio is the limit of tn+1 / tn where tn is the nth term of the Fibonacci Sequence

lim tn+1 / tn = lim tn / tn-1

(tn + tn-1) / tn = tn / tn-1

substitute tn-1i = S and tn = L and solve for L/S as above
